## 2025-11-23   Professional & Interview Wear for Silicon Valley CTO


  Silicon Valley has a "polished casual" dress code - you want to look sharp without being too formal.

  ---
  Key Brands:

  Premium (Investment Pieces):

  - Vince - elevated basics, soft fabrics
  - Theory - clean lines, modern cuts
  - Bonobos - great fit, tech-friendly aesthetic
  - Everlane - minimalist, quality basics
  - Lululemon ABC pants - technical fabric, professional look
  - Arc'teryx Veilance - technical menswear
  - Outlier - performance fabrics, tech aesthetic
  - AllBirds - comfortable, Silicon Valley staple

  Mid-Range (Best Value):

  - J.Crew - classic American style
  - Banana Republic - business casual
  - Uniqlo - affordable basics, good quality
  - Rhone - athletic-inspired professional wear
  - Ministry of Supply - performance workwear

  Shoes:

  - Common Projects - minimalist sneakers
  - Allbirds - wool sneakers (very Silicon Valley)
  - Cole Haan - dress shoes with comfort tech
  - Ecco - comfortable leather shoes

  ---
  Outfit Examples:

  Example 1: The "Elevated Casual" Interview

  - Top: Navy Vince crewneck sweater or Theory merino pullover
  - Bottom: Dark gray Bonobos chinos or Lululemon ABC pants (commission fit)
  - Shoes: White/gray Common Projects sneakers or Allbirds
  - Optional: Patagonia or Arc'teryx vest if it's cold
  - Why it works: Polished but not stuffy, shows you understand Valley culture

  Example 2: The "Polished Startup Founder"

  - Top: White or light blue Everlane Oxford shirt (untucked)
  - Bottom: Navy or charcoal Bonobos weekday warrior pants
  - Layer: Light gray Theory blazer (unstructured, no tie)
  - Shoes: Brown leather Cole Haan sneakers or loafers
  - Why it works: Professional enough for board meetings, casual enough for engineering team

  Example 3: The "Technical Leader"

  - Top: Fitted black or navy henley (Vince or Ministry of Supply)
  - Bottom: Dark denim (Levi's 511 or AG jeans - dark wash, no distressing)
  - Layer: Charcoal quarter-zip (Rhone or Lululemon)
  - Shoes: Clean white sneakers (Common Projects or Veja)
  - Why it works: Approachable, technical vibe, still executive-level

  Example 4: The "Board Meeting Ready"

  - Top: Crisp white Theory dress shirt
  - Bottom: Navy Theory suit pants (or full suit if presenting to investors)
  - Layer: Navy blazer (unstructured, can remove for casual feel)
  - Shoes: Brown leather Allen Edmonds or minimalist black leather
  - No tie (unless meeting with traditional finance/enterprise folks)
  - Why it works: Can dial up/down formality as needed

  Example 5: The "All-Day Comfort"

  - Top: Charcoal or navy Outlier merino tee
  - Bottom: Outlier slim dungarees or Lululaku ABC joggers (tapered)
  - Layer: Patagonia Better Sweater or Arc'teryx fleece
  - Shoes: Allbirds or minimalist sneakers
  - Why it works: Technical fabrics, all-day comfort, looks intentional

  ---
  Silicon Valley CTO Dress Code Rules:

  DO:

  ✅ Invest in fit over brand name
  ✅ Stick to neutrals (navy, gray, black, white)
  ✅ Wear clean, minimal sneakers (totally acceptable)
  ✅ Choose technical/performance fabrics (wrinkle-free, breathable)
  ✅ Layer smartly (you can remove blazer/vest to be more casual)
  ✅ Keep it simple and intentional

  DON'T:

  ❌ Wear a full suit with tie (unless investor meeting)
  ❌ Over-accessorize
  ❌ Wear logos/brands prominently
  ❌ Go too casual (no hoodies for interviews)
  ❌ Wear athletic wear (unless it's performance workwear style)
  ❌ Dress more formally than the CEO

  ---
  Interview-Specific Tips:

  For Startup Interview:
  - Go more casual (Example 3 or 5)
  - Sneakers are fine

  For Established Tech Company (Google, Meta, Apple):
  - Business casual (Example 1 or 2)
  - Can wear sneakers or leather shoes

  For Enterprise/B2B SaaS:
  - More polished (Example 2 or 4)
  - Consider leather shoes

  For VC/Board Presentations:
  - Most polished (Example 4)
  - Full blazer, possibly full suit

  ---
  Color Palette (Safe Choices):

  Tops: White, light blue, navy, charcoal, black
  Bottoms: Navy, charcoal, dark gray, black, dark denim
  Layers: Navy, charcoal, gray, black
  Shoes: White, gray, brown, black

  ---
  Quick Shopping List:

  Must-haves:
  1. 2-3 neutral sweaters (navy, gray, black)
  2. 2 pairs quality chinos (navy, charcoal)
  3. 1-2 Oxford shirts (white, light blue)
  4. 1 unstructured blazer (navy or charcoal)
  5. 1 pair clean white/minimal sneakers
  6. 1 pair leather shoes (brown or black)
  7. 1-2 performance henleys or tees

  ---
  Bottom line: Silicon Valley CTOs dress intentionally casual - quality fabrics, great fit, minimal style, no flashiness. Think "I care about looking
  put-together but I'm not trying too hard."
