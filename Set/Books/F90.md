## CHAPTER 8 - CREATE ALLIANCES

**DEFINE INFLUENCE OBJECTIVES**
- Influence Objectives:  Who, What, When
- Identify Winning & Blocking Alliances
- Map Influence Networks:  what are the key points of org interconnects?  who defers to whom?
  	Consider who has:  Expertise, Control of info, Connection to others, Access to resources, Personal loyalties.
- Draw diagrams
- Identify: Supporters, Opponents, and Persuadables.  Nurture supporters and ask force multiplication.  Alliance of convenience: enlist.  
  	Opponents & Persuadables:  Understand motives.   Persuadable may be:  Indifferent, Undecided, Politically aiming for winning side.
- Understand situational pressures too
- Build personnel motivations table

**CRAFT INFLUENCE STRATEGIES**
- Consultation promotes buy-in
- Framing agenda on person-by-person basis:  Logos, Ethos, Pathos.  Repetition of key themes.

**FRAMING ARGUMENTS**:  Use Logos, Ethos, Pathos arguments

**STRATEGIES**
- CHOICE SHAPING: Make it hard to say no
- SOCIAL INFLUENCING: Others bought in first will greatly help
- CONSISTENCY: with their beliefs and values; prior commitments/decisions; repay obligations; preserve other's reputation
- INCREMENTALISM: Getting people to take baby steps.  Perhaps just agreement to study problem
- SEQUENCING: Being strategic about order in trying to build influence.
  	Mix: 1-on-1 for lay of land.  face-2-face group meetings for decisions and commitments
- ACTION FORCING: Meeting/Tele cons, Review-sessions, Deadlines.  (forcing function for laggards to get on board)
