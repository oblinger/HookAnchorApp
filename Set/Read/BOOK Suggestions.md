### 2024 - what are the titles in the culture series


The Culture series by Iain M. Banks is a highly acclaimed science fiction series known for its exploration of advanced societies, artificial intelligence, and moral and philosophical questions. The series consists of the following titles:

14. **"Consider Phlebas" (1987)**: The first published book in the series, introducing the Culture, a post-scarcity, space-faring society.
    
15. **"The Player of Games" (1988)**: Focuses on a master game player from the Culture and his involvement in the complex games of a different, empire-like society.
    
16. **"Use of Weapons" (1990)**: Centers around a mercenary and his complex relationship with the Culture.
    
17. **"The State of the Art" (1991)**: A collection of short stories and a novella, with the title story involving the Culture's contact with Earth.
    
18. **"Excession" (1996)**: Deals with the Culture's encounter with an enigmatic, ancient alien artifact.
    
19. **"Inversions" (1998)**: A novel that hints at the involvement of the Culture in a medieval-like society, though it's more subtle in its references to the broader universe.
    
20. **"Look to Windward" (2000)**: Set in the Culture universe, this novel explores themes of war, loss, and revenge.
    
21. **"Matter" (2008)**: Involves a prince from a medieval-level society who seeks help from the Culture.
    
22. **"Surface Detail" (2010)**: Deals with themes of virtual realities and afterlives, and the moral implications of such technologies.
    
23. **"The Hydrogen Sonata" (2012)**: The final book published before Banks' death, this novel explores a civilization preparing to sublimate to a higher plane of existence.
