
> that are the best job aggregator sites

⏺ Top job aggregator sites:

  Major Aggregators:
  - Indeed - largest, pulls from many sources
  - LinkedIn Jobs - professional network + aggregation
  - Glassdoor - jobs + company reviews/salaries
  - ZipRecruiter - fast application process
  - Monster - long-established aggregator

  Specialized:
  - AngelList/Wellfound - startups/tech
  - Dice - tech/IT focused
  - FlexJobs - remote/flexible work
  - SimplyHired - clean interface, good filtering

  Meta-aggregators:
  - Google Jobs - searches across multiple sites
  - Adzuna - salary insights + aggregation

  Direct Company Sites:
  Many companies post exclusively on their own careers pages, so checking target companies directly is also important.

  Indeed and LinkedIn tend to have the broadest coverage, while Google Jobs can surface listings from smaller sites.



> I am interested in applying to jobs the day that the job posting is first posted, what service can I use to monitor for this

⏺ For immediate job posting alerts:

  Built-in Alert Services:
  - LinkedIn Job Alerts - can be set to daily/weekly email notifications
  - Indeed Job Alerts - email notifications for new matching jobs
  - Google Jobs - has alert functionality
  - Glassdoor Alerts - email notifications for new postings

  Advanced Monitoring:
  - RSS feeds - some job sites offer RSS feeds you can monitor
  - IFTTT/Zapier - can create automated workflows to monitor job sites
  - Custom web scrapers - tools like Scrapy or browser automation

  Specialized Services:
  - JobScan - has alert features
  - Jobseer - job alert aggregation service
  - Job Alert services - third-party services that monitor multiple sites

  Most Effective Approach:
  Set up multiple alerts across LinkedIn, Indeed, and Google Jobs with very specific keywords and location filters. LinkedIn tends to be fastest for professional roles, while
   Indeed catches more diverse postings.

  For maximum speed, also follow target companies directly on LinkedIn and enable notifications for their posts.
