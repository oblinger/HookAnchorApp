
## Short List

### Cure A Disease
#### Identify Disease Markers
#### Explain Disease Functioning
#### Describe the treatment regimen to cure the disease & demonstrate it works
#### Describe preventative steps to avoid the disease & demonstrate it works




## Long List
1. **Disease Eradication** – Investigate the progression and underlying causes of a pathological condition within a specific alien organism, then devise actions (treatments) to restore normal function. This often involves identifying the pathogenic processes, predicting their evolution, and neutralizing them.

2. **Disease Prevention** – Characterize the factors that initiate illness in an alien ecosystem and design prophylactic measures (vaccines, targeted prophylaxis) that prevent the onset of disease before symptoms manifest.

3. **Growth Rate Enhancement** – Pinpoint the biological processes that regulate the growth of particular organs or organisms, then manipulate actions (nutritional inputs, hormonal adjustments) to increase biomass or accelerate development.

4. **Population Regulation** – Stabilize the population size of an alien species by controlling birth rate, death rate, or resource competition. Balancing the carrying capacity of an ecosystem is central to this challenge.

5. **Toxin Neutralization** – Isolate the alien molecules and processes involved in toxicity, then develop strategies (chemical binding, metabolic degradation) that reduce or eliminate the harmful effect of those toxins.

6. **Nutrient Optimization** – Discover how certain biomolecules are absorbed and metabolized by an alien organ or organism, then orchestrate inputs and environmental factors to ensure optimal nutrient uptake.

7. **Organ Regeneration** – Explore how damaged or removed organs might be naturally regenerated via the alien physiology’s organ-formation pathways, and devise targeted interventions (stimulating growth factors, reinforcing structural scaffolds) to speed or guide regeneration.

8. **Metabolic Pathway Re-Engineering** – Map the alien cell’s metabolic processes and reconfigure the rates and routes of molecular conversions to achieve a certain output (e.g., high energy yield, minimized byproduct waste).

9. **Behavioral Conditioning** – Use feedback mechanisms at the neural or organ system level to modify how an alien organism reacts to stimuli, with the goal of establishing or eradicating specific behaviors (feeding, social interaction, avoidance).

10. **Symbiosis Creation** – Identify two or more organisms whose mutual exchange of biomolecules or organ-level services can be engineered into a stable, beneficial relationship that didn’t previously exist.

11. **Controlled Evolution** – Design selective pressures in simulated generations of an alien organism to favor traits of interest (adaptation to heat, resistance to toxins), effectively driving evolution on an accelerated timescale.

12. **Ecosystem Homeostasis** – Adjust multiple interacting species and environmental parameters (temperature, resource distribution) so the whole alien ecosystem remains in a balanced, near-steady state without collapse or overgrowth of any part.

13. **Cross-Species Organ Transplantation** – Determine if organs from one alien species can be surgically or functionally integrated into a different species by overcoming immune rejection or adjusting the organ’s bioprocess interface.

14. **Pathway Inhibition Studies** – Investigate specific biochemical pathways (e.g., a lethal infection cycle) and identify precise intervention points or regulatory molecules to halt those processes before they cause systemic harm.

15. **Immunological Enhancement** – Determine how an alien immune-like system detects and responds to foreign biomolecules, then boost its capabilities via interventions that strengthen recognition or accelerate response times.

16. **Enzyme Design** – Evaluate which alien enzymes or enzyme-analogs control critical reaction steps in biomolecule conversion, then adjust or engineer these enzymes to improve process efficiency or specificity.

17. **Cloning and Replication** – Develop procedures to replicate entire alien organisms with high fidelity, testing for stable inheritance of biomolecular pathways and organ structures across successive generations.

18. **Transgenerational Trait Transfer** – Identify inherited features in alien biology and manipulate them to ensure certain traits (e.g., disease resistance) reliably pass to progeny, possibly incorporating novel gene-like elements.

19. **Bioreactor Development** – Use alien microorganisms or modified organs as living factories to produce high-value biomolecules (e.g., unique proteins, specialized toxins) in controlled environments.

20. **Ecosystem Restoration** – Reverse damage in an alien habitat by tracing the chain of ecosystem imbalances (resource depletion, invasive species) and implementing species reintroductions or nutrient rebalancing until stability is regained.

21. **Multi-Level Signaling Analysis** – Investigate how signals propagate from molecules up through cells, organs, and entire organisms, then exploit or interfere with those signaling cascades to produce desired phenotypic changes.

22. **Adaptive Morphology** – Expose an alien organism to changing environments or controlled stresses, measuring and promoting morphological adjustments (e.g., limb extension, hardened exoskeleton) in real-time.

23. **Biomaterial Harvesting** – Characterize alien structures (shell, exoskeleton, secreted fibers) that can be extracted and repurposed, optimizing harvest techniques to maximize yield while minimizing harm to the source species.

24. **Organ Circuit Rewiring** – Determine the dependencies and feedback loops among an alien’s organs, then restructure those relationships (e.g., replace a hormone source with a synthetic alternative) without disrupting system integrity.

25. **Host-Parasite Equilibrium** – Study parasitic relationships to see how to maintain or shift them from harmful to balanced states, monitoring the interplay of parasite virulence and host immunity to avoid detrimental outcomes.
