# 10 Movies to Watch with Your 6-Year-Old Son

## 1. **The Incredibles** (2004)
**Why:** Superhero family action, great humor, positive messages about family and being yourself. Not too scary, lots of fun action sequences.

## 2. **Paddington** (2014)
**Why:** Heartwarming, funny, beautiful visuals. Teaches kindness and acceptance. Perfect for this age.

## 3. **Moana** (2016)
**Why:** Adventure, catchy songs, strong characters, beautiful animation. Great story about courage and finding your path.

## 4. **Toy Story** (1995)
**Why:** Classic, relatable characters, themes of friendship and growing up. Perfect gateway to the whole series.

## 5. **How to Train Your Dragon** (2010)
**Why:** Dragons, friendship, adventure, stunning visuals. Empowering story about being different.

## 6. **Coco** (2017)
**Why:** Beautiful music, family themes, teaches about culture and remembering loved ones. Touching but age-appropriate.

## 7. **Ratatouille** (2007)
**Why:** Creative story, funny, teaches following your dreams. Great visuals of Paris and cooking.

## 8. **The Lion King** (1994)
**Why:** Classic coming-of-age story, amazing music, themes of responsibility and courage. Emotional but important lessons.

## 9. **Finding Nemo** (2003)
**Why:** Adventure, humor, beautiful ocean visuals. Strong father-son themes, teaches perseverance.

## 10. **Spirited Away** (2001) (Studio Ghibli)
**Why:** Magical, imaginative, teaches bravery and kindness. Beautiful animation. May need some explanation but wonderful for expanding imagination.

---

## **Bonus Picks:**

- **Big Hero 6** - Robots, friendship, dealing with loss in age-appropriate way
- **Zootopia** - Buddy cop adventure, teaches about stereotypes and believing in yourself
- **The Iron Giant** - Friendship, sci-fi, touching story about peace vs violence
- **WALL-E** - Mostly visual storytelling, environmental themes, sweet love story
- **Inside Out** - Teaches about emotions in a fun way

---

## **Tips for Watching:**

 Start with lighter ones (Paddington, Moana, Toy Story)
 Be ready to pause and answer questions
 Some emotional moments (Lion King, Coco) - good for teaching empathy
 Make popcorn, make it special!
