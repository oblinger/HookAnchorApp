


## Friday Night

####  Pier 39 Evening Fun
  - See the sea lions (they're there 24/7 and lit up at night!)
  - San Francisco Carousel
  - Street performers and musicians
  - Aquarium of the Bay (open until 8pm)
  - Dinner with a view at family-friendly restaurants

#### Exploratorium After Dark (if still open)
  - Check their evening hours - they sometimes have special Friday night events
  - Hands-on science exhibits perfect for curious 6-year-olds
  - Located at Pier 15

#### Ghirardelli Square
  - Get hot chocolate or ice cream sundaes
  - Beautiful evening views of the bay
  - Often has live music on weekends
  - Easy walk from Fisherman's Wharf

#### Cable Car Ride at Twilight
  - Kids love riding the cable cars
  - Beautiful city views as the sun sets
  - End at Union Square to see the lights

#### Chinatown Evening Stroll
  - Dragon Gate is beautifully lit at night
  - Get dim sum or noodles for dinner
  - Browse the shops (many open late)
  - Fortune cookies at Golden Gate Fortune Cookie Factory

####  Quick Evening Options:
  - Crissy Field: Watch the sunset with Golden Gate Bridge views
  - Ferry Building: Friday evening farmers market (if still running) plus dinner options
  - Golden Gate Park: Playground time before dark at Koret Children's Quarter

#### Additional Friday Night Ideas
  - Children's Creativity Museum: Digital art & animation studios (check evening hours)
  - Yerba Buena Gardens: Playground & carousel, often events on Fridays
  - Metreon: Kid-friendly movies and arcade games
  - Ocean Beach Bonfire: Reserve a fire pit for s'mores at sunset
  - California Academy of Sciences: Thursday NightLife is 21+, but check for family nights

## Friday Night near 94110

#### Balmy Alley Murals (24th Street)
  - Famous Mission District mural alley, beautiful when lit up
  - Free outdoor art gallery perfect for evening stroll
  - Between 24th St & 25th St, just off Treat Ave
  - Many nearby family-friendly restaurants

#### Urban Putt Indoor Mini Golf
  - 14 unique San Francisco-themed holes
  - Kid-friendly until 8pm (becomes 21+ later)
  - 1096 S Van Ness Ave (at 22nd St)
  - Reserve tee times online

#### Precita Eyes Mural Arts Center
  - Evening mural walks on some Fridays
  - Indoor gallery with rotating exhibits
  - 2981 24th St (at Harrison)
  - Check website for family workshop schedules

#### Mission Bowling Club
  - 6 lanes, retro atmosphere
  - Kids welcome until 9pm
  - 3176 17th St (at S Van Ness)
  - Food menu with kid options

#### Dolores Park Playground
  - Great playground with city views
  - Usually active until dusk
  - 20th & Church St
  - Bi-Rite Creamery nearby for ice cream

#### The Randall Museum
  - Model train exhibit kids love
  - Open some Friday evenings for special events
  - 199 Museum Way (Corona Heights)
  - Free admission, check evening hours

#### Mission District Food Tour
  - La Palma Mexicatessen for fresh tortillas
  - Tartine Bakery for pastries (5:30pm closing)
  - Mitchell's Ice Cream (until 10pm)
  - Mission Pie for dinner and dessert

## Walking Areas near 94110

#### Mission District Walking Areas
  - 24th Street Corridor - Vibrant shops, restaurants, street art
  - Valencia Street - Hip cafes, bookstores, vintage shops
  - Balmy Alley - Famous mural alley between 24th & 25th
  - Clarion Alley - Another great mural spot (17th & Valencia)
  - Mission Cultural District - 24th & Mission, authentic Latin culture

#### Adjacent Neighborhoods (10-15 min walk)
  - Dolores Park - Great for picnics, city views, people watching
  - Dolores Street - Beautiful palm-lined boulevard
  - Castro District - Historic neighborhood, colorful Victorian houses
  - 24th Street Noe Valley - Charming "stroller town" shops
  - Church Street - Quiet residential with cafes

#### Slightly Further (20-30 min walk)
  - Cortland Avenue (Bernal Heights) - Village-like main street
  - Bernal Heights Park - 360-degree city views from the hill
  - Precita Park - Nice neighborhood park
  - South Park - Small oval park surrounded by startups
  - AT&T Park area - Waterfront walks along the bay
  - 18th Street (Potrero Hill) - Main commercial strip with great views
  - Connecticut Street - Steep but scenic residential walking

#### Best for Evening/Family Walks
  - Dolores Park to Castro
  - Valencia Street from 16th to 24th
  - Mission Creek Greenway (towards the bay)
